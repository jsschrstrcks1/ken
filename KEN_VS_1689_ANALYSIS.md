## Areas of Perfect 1689 Alignment

### 1. Sola Scriptura (Scripture Alone)
**1689 Position:** Scripture is the only rule of faith and practice.
**Ken's Preaching:** 
- Luke 23: Heavy critique of man-made rules (Pharisees adding to Torah, Catholic papal authority adding to Scripture)
- Consistently shows Scripture correcting human tradition
- Refuses political proof-texting ("like when the presidential candidate accidentally referenced 1 John 2:4")
- **ALIGNMENT:** ✅ Perfect

### 2. Sovereignty of God in Salvation
**1689 Position:** God is sovereign; grace is not earned but granted by God's will.
**Ken's Preaching:**
- Luke 19: "What is impossible with man is possible with God" (sovereign grace finds Zacchaeus)
- "Not a better argument. Not a louder preacher. Sovereign grace that stops under the branch and calls a name."
- Zacchaeus "seeks" but Jesus must "call"—initiative is God's
- **ALIGNMENT:** ✅ Perfect

### 3. Substitutionary Atonement
**1689 Position:** Christ's death is the substitutionary payment for sin.
**Ken's Preaching:**
- Luke 23: "Jesus took the punishment Barabbas deserved"
- "Barabbas was given mercy and grace, and a second chance. Just like us."
- The Barabbas choice = picture of substitution (Jesus in Barabbas's place)
- **ALIGNMENT:** ✅ Perfect

### 4. Justification by Faith Alone (Not Works)
**1689 Position:** Salvation comes by faith, not by works of the law.
**Ken's Preaching:**
- Luke 19: Zacchaeus's restitution (giving to poor, restoring fourfold) comes AFTER salvation, not before
- "Today salvation has come to this house" — spoken by Jesus, not earned by Zacchaeus's future actions
- Money is the "obstacle" not the "price" of salvation
- **ALIGNMENT:** ✅ Perfect

### 5. Total Depravity (Man Cannot Save Himself)
**1689 Position:** Man is so corrupted by sin he cannot merit or earn salvation.
**Ken's Preaching:**
- Luke 19: Rich young ruler "went away very sad" — his wealth made obedience impossible for him
- Zacchaeus is "impossible" (chief tax collector, blood money, traitor) yet Jesus finds him
- Man's efforts fail; only God's grace works
- **ALIGNMENT:** ✅ Perfect

### 6. Particular Redemption (Elect Chosen by God)
**1689 Position:** Christ died specifically for the elect; not universally for all.
**Ken's Preaching:**
- Luke 19: "Today salvation has come to this house" — spoken to one man, at a specific moment
- Jesus "seeks and saves the lost" (particular action, not universal offer)
- "Your people group of the week" (prayer for specific unreached people, implying God's sovereign calling)
- **ALIGNMENT:** ✅ Perfect

### 7. Church Discipline & Pastoral Care
**1689 Position:** Church must care for souls; pastor is a shepherd.
**Ken's Preaching:**
- Constant refrain: "Pray for the Sundanese," "Father, we pray..."
- Emphasis on pastor as shepherd calling sheep by name ("calls a name")
- Luke 19: Jesus looking, calling, seeking — pastoral heart
- **ALIGNMENT:** ✅ Perfect

### 8. God's Character: Holy, Jealous, Loving
**1689 Position:** God is holy (cannot overlook sin), jealous (demands exclusive worship), and loving (gave Himself).
**Ken's Preaching:**
- Luke 23: "He is a jealous God. He doesn't want to be worshipped alongside 2 million fake gods."
- "Christ was completely innocent" (holiness of God reflected in spotless Son)
- "Seek and save the lost" (love that pursues)
- **ALIGNMENT:** ✅ Perfect

## Areas Where Ken Should Explicitly Teach (Not Conflicts, Just Gaps)

### 1. Believer's Baptism (Credobaptism)
**1689 Core:** Baptism is for believers only; requires personal faith profession.
**Ken's Preaching:** Not found in sampled sermons.
**Assessment:** Likely in full agreement (all his theology fits). Just not a sermon topic yet.
**Action:** Teaching on believer's baptism would naturally follow from his soteriology.

### 2. Congregational Polity (Not Hierarchical)
**1689 Core:** Church governance is congregational, not episcopal or papal.
**Ken's Preaching:** Implied in anti-papal critiques (Luke 23: Catholic hierarchy as corruption).
**Assessment:** Fully aligned; would benefit from explicit teaching.
**Action:** Sermon on church leadership structure / elder plurality.

### 3. God's Eternal Decrees (Predestination, Election)
**1689 Core:** God's decrees are eternal, not reactive; election is certain.
**Ken's Preaching:** Implied in sovereignty language; not explicitly detailed.
**Assessment:** Fully aligned; needs doctrinal depth.
**Action:** Systematic teaching on predestination (without fatalism).

### 4. The Two Covenants (Law & Gospel Distinction)
**1689 Core:** Law and Gospel are distinct; Gospel is the power that fulfills the Law.
**Ken's Preaching:** Implicit in many places (Pharisees vs. Jesus, rules vs. grace).
**Assessment:** Fully aligned; would sharpen with explicit teaching.
**Action:** Sermon on Law & Gospel distinction.

## Areas Needing Nuance (Not Conflicts, Just Clarification)

### 1. Wealth & Kingdom
**1689 Position:** Wealth is not inherently evil; earthly goods are God's gifts. But love of money is root of all evil.
**Ken's Preaching:**
- Luke 19: "Something between you and God" — could be money, or could be anything
- Luke 23: Critique of power-seeking, not of wealth per se
- **Issue:** Ken rightly emphasizes idolatry of wealth but doesn't explicitly teach that wealth itself is morally neutral (gift from God that must not become idol)
- **Clarification needed:** "Wealth is good (God's gift); loving wealth is evil (sin). The issue is the heart, not the balance sheet."

### 2. Civil Government & Authority
**1689 Position:** God establishes civil authority; believers must submit (Romans 13), but God's law trumps man's law.
**Ken's Preaching:**
- Luke 23: Israel oppressed by Rome; Ken shows the people's desire for freedom
- Roman authority is implicit; Jesus submits to unjust trial
- **Gap:** No explicit teaching on Christian responsibility to civil government (should obey law, pay taxes, pray for leaders, but refuse immoral commands)
- **Clarification needed:** Not a conflict; just a gap.

### 3. Common Grace (God's Kindness to All, Elect & Non-Elect)
**1689 Position:** God shows kindness to all creation (rain on just and unjust); this is not saving grace.
**Ken's Preaching:**
- Not explicitly addressed in sampled sermons
- **Gap:** Teaching on how God's general goodness differs from His saving grace would enrich the theology
- **Clarification needed:** "God is kind to all (common grace); saving grace is different (particular grace). Both come from His character."

## Potential Tensions (That Don't Actually Exist)

### 1. Free Will vs. Predestination (False Tension)
**1689 Position:** God is sovereign; man is responsible. Both are true (antinomy, not paradox).
**Ken's Preaching:**
- Luke 19: Zacchaeus "seeks" (human action) AND "Jesus calls" (God's action) — both are real
- Luke 23: Israel "chooses" Barabbas (human responsibility) AND God's plan unfolds (divine sovereignty)
- **Verdict:** ✅ Ken holds both without collapsing one into the other. This is mature theology.

### 2. Grace vs. Obedience (False Tension)
**1689 Position:** Grace is free; obedience is the grateful response (not condition).
**Ken's Preaching:**
- Luke 19: Zacchaeus "received him joyfully" (grace received freely), THEN gave to the poor (obedience as fruit)
- Order is clear: grace first, then works
- **Verdict:** ✅ Perfect distinction. No false gospel of works.
