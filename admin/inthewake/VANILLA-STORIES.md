## Overview

"Vanilla stories" are generic, template-based content that lacks:
- Ship-specific details
- Service recovery narrative
- Emotional depth
- Real passenger experiences

These stories must be updated with authentic, engaging content before ships can pass validation.

## Vanilla Story Characteristics

### Institutional Authors (Current Remaining Type)
- "Costa Fleet Chronicle"
- "European Entertainment Review"
- "Renaissance Heritage Society"
- "Tuscan Culinary Institute"
- "Art History Journal"
- "Explora Journeys Fleet Development Team"

### Original Template Pattern (Now Eliminated)
- Titles: "First Impressions of [Ship Name]", "Dining Aboard [Ship Name]"
- Author: "Community Contributor"
- Length: 100-150 words
- No emotional content

---

## What Makes a Story NOT Vanilla

### Quality Story Characteristics:
- Specific ship venues mentioned (Le Bistro, Cagney's, Polo Grill, Colosseo)
- Real author name with location ("Catherine R., Chicago, IL")
- Emotional narrative arc (hook → setup → ship in action → emotional pivot → lesson)
- Service recovery story or crew interaction
- 400-800+ word count
- Unique persona perspective (solo, widow, veteran, family, etc.)
- Faith-scented moments (natural, not forced)
- Internal links to ship page features
